Hephaestus

God of Fire and Metallurgy

Hephaestus was the brilliant blacksmith of the Olympian Gods, with his workshop beneath volcanoes. Hephaestus had a lame foot, causing him to be known as the less than perfect god. His origins are still rather obscure, but it’s believed that he was born from Hera, without a father, out of jealousy of Zeus birthing Athena. Due to his disability, it was said that either Hera or Zeus threw him off of Olympus, landing in the island of Lemnos. He was cared for by Thetis and Eurynome. He would build his workshop in the island’s volcano.

Once Hephaestus made his way back to Olympus, he captured his mother, Hera, taking revenge for her throwing him out of Olympus. In exchange for Hera getting released, she promised Aphrodite’s hand in marriage. However, Aphrodite had many affairs, ending the marriage quickly. Hephaestus caught them during the act and called upon Olympus to witness them.

What Hephaestus had made:

  • The sceptre and aegis of Zeus
  • The helmet and sandals of Hermes
  • Secret locking doors for Hera’s chambers
  • Aphrodite’s girdle
  • Achilles’ armour
  • Helios’ chariot
  • Heracles’ bronze clappers
  • Diomedes’ cuirass
  • The lovely woman Pandora, sculpted from clay
  • Gold maids who could speak and were intelligent
  • Bronze Talos gifted to King Minos of Crete
  • Watchdogs for Alcinous
